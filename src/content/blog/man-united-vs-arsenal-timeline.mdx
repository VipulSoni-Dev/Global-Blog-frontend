# Manchester United F.C. vs Arsenal F.C. Timeline

![Manchester United Crest](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/7a/Manchester_United_FC_crest.svg)
![Arsenal Crest](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/5/53/Arsenal_FC.svg)

Few rivalries in English football burn brighter than **Manchester United vs Arsenal**.  
From legendary managers Sir Alex Ferguson and Arsène Wenger, to unforgettable goals by Thierry Henry, Wayne Rooney, and Cristiano Ronaldo, this fixture has shaped the history of the **Premier League**.

---

## ⚽ Early Clashes (Pre-Premier League Era)

- **1919–1950s** – The rivalry starts brewing in the old First Division.  
- **1979 FA Cup Final** – Arsenal defeated United 3–2 in one of the most thrilling finals of the era.  

---

## 🔥 The Ferguson–Wenger Years (1996–2005)

This was the **golden age of the rivalry**. Every meeting was a battle for supremacy in the Premier League.

- **1996** – Arsène Wenger arrives at Arsenal, bringing a new football philosophy.  
- **1999** – Ryan Giggs’ stunning FA Cup semi-final goal knocked Arsenal out in extra time.  
- **2002** – Arsenal clinched the Premier League title at Old Trafford with a 1–0 win (Sylvain Wiltord).  
- **2003** – The infamous “Battle of Old Trafford” where Arsenal players confronted Ruud van Nistelrooy after his missed penalty.  
- **2004** – United ended Arsenal’s **49-match unbeaten run** with a 2–0 win at Old Trafford.  

---

## 🌍 Modern Era (2006–Present)

With new managers and global stars, the fixture remains a highlight of the season:

- **2009** – Cristiano Ronaldo and Park Ji-sung destroyed Arsenal in the Champions League semi-final.  
- **2011** – United humiliated Arsenal 8–2 at Old Trafford, with Rooney scoring a hat-trick.  
- **2018** – A high-octane 2–2 draw with spectacular saves from David de Gea.  
- **2023** – Arsenal’s resurgence under Mikel Arteta brought back the fire; thrilling 3–2 victory at the Emirates with Eddie Nketiah’s late winner.  

---

## 🏆 Legends of the Rivalry

- **Manchester United**: Ryan Giggs, Roy Keane, Cristiano Ronaldo, Wayne Rooney, Eric Cantona.  
- **Arsenal**: Thierry Henry, Patrick Vieira, Dennis Bergkamp, Tony Adams, Cesc Fàbregas.  

---
