# 🏇 York Races: Where Heritage Meets High Stakes  

York Racecourse—fondly known as *The Knavesmire*—is one of the crown jewels of British racing. From **Roman times** to the present day, York has been a battleground for legendary horses, fashion icons, and multimillion-pound prize purses.  

---

## 📜 A Legacy Etched in Time
- Racing in York dates back to **1730**, when the Clifton Ings floods forced organizers to move to today’s Knavesmire.  
- **1751** saw the launch of the *Great Subscription Purses*, elite four-mile races that tested stamina.  
- By **1843**, the *Ebor Handicap* emerged, now worth **£500,000+** — the richest flat handicap in Europe.  

---

## 🌟 The Ebor Festival: Yorkshire’s Crowny Jewel
Each August, York hosts the **Ebor Festival**, four electrifying days of flat racing and fashion:  

- **Wednesday**: *Juddmonte International Stakes* — global stars clash.  
- **Thursday**: *Ladies’ Day* — elegance meets the *Yorkshire Oaks*.  
- **Friday**: *Nunthorpe Stakes* — blistering 5-furlong speed.  
- **Saturday**: *Ebor Handicap* finale — with purses exceeding **£300,000**.
